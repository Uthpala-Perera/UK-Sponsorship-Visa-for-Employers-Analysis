# UK Sponsorship Visa for Employers Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes UK Sponsorship Visa application data for employers using interactive Power BI dashboards. The goal is to uncover trends in study and work visa applications, nationality distributions, industry demand, and quarter-over-quarter growth patterns to support better understanding of UK immigration sponsorship dynamics.

## Tools & Technologies

* Power BI
* Data Cleaning & Transformation
* Data Visualization
* DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
* SQL Server

## Key Features

* **Overview Dashboard** — High-level summary of total applications, study-to-work ratio, and latest quarter figures
* **Study Sponsorship Analysis** — Trends in study visa applications by quarter, institution type, and nationality
* **Work Sponsorship Analysis** — Work visa application trends by quarter, industry, category of leave, and nationality
* **Cumulative Application Tracking** — Quarter-by-quarter cumulative growth for both study and work visa streams
* **Geographical Region Breakdown** — Applications segmented by global region (Asia South, Asia East, Africa Sub-Saharan, etc.)
* **Nationality-Level Drill-Down** — Top source countries ranked by total application volume
* **QoQ Growth Monitoring** — Quarter-over-quarter growth rates tracked for both visa categories

## Dataset
UK Home Office – Sponsorship Visa Application Data

## Key Insights

### Overview
* Over **16 million total applications** recorded across the dataset
* The **study-to-work ratio** stands at **1.74**, reflecting significantly higher study visa demand
* The latest quarter recorded approximately **328,000 applications**
* Work visas account for **36.46%** of all applications; study visas account for **63.54%**

### Study Sponsorship
* **10 million total study applications** recorded
* Study applications account for **0.64 (64%) share** of total applications
* **Study QoQ growth** peaked at **1692.82%** at its highest recorded quarter
* **China** is the top source country with **1,614,458** study applications
* **India** follows with **782,071** study applications
* **Non-Russell Group** institutions attract the highest volume of study applicants
* **Higher Education** institutions dominate by institution type group, far outpacing all other institution categories
* Study application volumes show notable peaks and troughs across quarters, with visible spikes around 2011–2012

### Work Sponsorship
* **6 million total work applications** recorded
* Work applications account for a **0.36 (36%) share** of total applications
* **Work QoQ growth** peaked at **2728.96%** at its highest recorded quarter
* **India** is the top source country for work visas with **872,585** applications
* **United States** ranks second with **394,420** work applications
* **Human Health** is the leading industry by total work applications, followed by Arts & Entertainment and Information sectors
* **Worker (Previous)** is the dominant category of leave, significantly ahead of the Temporary Worker category
* Work application volumes remained relatively flat through 2010–2012 before accelerating

### Geographical Insights
* **Asia South** is the largest source region for total applications, followed closely by **Asia East**
* **Africa Sub-Saharan** and **America North** rank third and fourth respectively
* **Middle East** and **Asia South East** also contribute meaningful application volumes
